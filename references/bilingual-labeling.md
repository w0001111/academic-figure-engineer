# Bilingual Labeling Rules

Use these rules when working with Chinese and English manuscripts.

## Default language

- English journal submission: figure labels and captions should be in English.
- Chinese draft or internal review: Chinese labels are acceptable.
- Bilingual review package: provide English figure labels and both English + Chinese captions.

## Terminology consistency

Maintain a term table when the project is bilingual.

Example:

| Chinese | English |
|---|---|
| 交通-能源耦合 | traffic-energy coupling |
| 无功补偿 | reactive power compensation |
| 闲置车辆 | idle vehicles |
| 调度策略 | dispatch policy |
| 工程约束 | engineering constraints |

Do not translate the same term in multiple ways within one figure package.

## Label length

- English labels: prefer 2–6 words.
- Chinese labels: prefer 4–10 Chinese characters.
- If a concept needs a long explanation, use a callout or caption rather than a crowded box.

## Caption policy

For bilingual projects, deliver:

1. English caption for submission;
2. Chinese caption for internal review;
3. a short terminology note if important terms are ambiguous.

## Do not mix languages casually

Avoid mixing Chinese and English in the same node unless the manuscript itself uses a bilingual technical term or acronym.
