# Example: Traffic-Energy Coupling Framework

## Input summary

A study coordinates idle electric vehicles, charging stations, traffic travel times, and grid reactive power compensation. Vehicles must satisfy mobility constraints while stations provide active charging service and idle reactive support.

## Central message

The figure should explain the coupling among traffic mobility, station service capacity, and grid-side active/reactive power support.

## Suggested figure type

Traffic-energy coupling mechanism or overall research framework.

## Layout

Use four layers:

1. Traffic layer: road network, travel time, vehicle movement.
2. Charging service layer: station assignment, charging demand, waiting/service constraints.
3. Power layer: active power demand, reactive compensation, station engineering limits.
4. Decision/control layer: dispatch policy, optimization objective, feedback from realized states.

## Notes

- Cross-layer arrows must be labeled with coupling variables.
- Do not imply grid power flow simulation unless the paper actually performs it.
- Distinguish physical execution from computational optimization.
